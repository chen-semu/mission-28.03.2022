Macabi Los angeles

Maccabi Los angeles (also known as macabi Urban Kiryat Malachi) is an Israeli football team from the city of Kiryat Malachi, competing in the Second Division South. The colors of the club are orange, black and white.

The club was founded in 1962 and began participating in the Third Division in the 1962/1963 season and finished in fourth place. Already in its debut season, it was hosted for the first time in the derby of Kiryat Malachi, in "Mashhaks Am Kahal" held in Moshav Beit Ezra on January 12, 1963, and ended in a 1-1 draw. Maccabi took the lead in the 25th minute (Ephraim Jury). Hapoel equalized in the 88th minute (Amos Saban). For 31 consecutive seasons, the team played in the third division, Maccabi's prominent players in its early years were: goalkeeper Reuven Cohen, Ilan Krief, Yehuda Nadav, Ephraim Jury, Cousin and Makhlouf Dahan, the only one who remained at the Cohen club over the years, and served in many positions. In the 1964/1965 season, the team finished in first place in the Third Division Central, but failed in the playoffs and finished last in the Southern House
